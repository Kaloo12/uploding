The performance group: this group normally does free performances in public areas for the community once a week; during the school breaks, it travels to different parts of Ethiopia. The performance ensembles go on foreign tours as well. The boys and girls who are permanent members of the performing troupes and are ages 8 to 28 in Addis Ababa, are all urged to continue with their regular education and to enrol in the circus's tutorial classes. The majority are from very low-income families; they are provided with meals after long practises and performances, receive a transportation allowance, and have their medical needs met.The members are drawn from a variety of backgrounds and faith traditions. The majority of them have been with the Circus since its inception.Additionally, lodging is offered.

Fekat Circus School is a bouncing and colorful place, located in the historical centre of Addis Ababa. The space vibrates with children and youngsters jumping, juggling, tumbling, and dancing. The main circus disciplines taught are acrobatics, balance acts (hand stand, ladder, rola-bola), Chinese pole, contortion, human pyramid, different juggling arts (balls, clubs, diabolo, hat, hula-hoop, ring, poas, etc.), and aerial arts. We have added creative lab as a new activity in the school programme.
